# Meteor shower event

> When to read this: before changing the meteor schedule, siting, sizes, blast, crater cluster, or trajectory math, or when debugging where/when a meteor lands. Source of truth: `src/server/meteor_shower.rs` (authority) and `src/world/meteor_shower.rs` (shared math). The client chrome is owned by [docs/ui-and-client.md](ui-and-client.md).

The meteor shower is a recurring server-scheduled world event: two meteors of varied sizes streak in over staggered flights, strike clearance-checked sites spread across the map's outer ring, damage players and clear resource nodes in each size-scaled blast radius, and scatter contested clusters of rich meteorite nodes that despawn if unmined. **The event is deliberately unannounced**: there is no countdown UI, no map marker, and no chat line; the sky fireballs and the audio ARE the announcement. The only warning chrome is the per-meteor escalating "evacuate" banner, shown only while the local player stands inside that meteor's size-scaled danger radius. The whole design rests on one wire message plus pure shared math; nothing about a meteor is streamed.

## Announce-only wire contract

The entire event ships as one broadcast: `ServerMessage::MeteorShower { meteors: Vec<MeteorStrike> }` (src/protocol/messages.rs - ServerMessage::MeteorShower), where each `MeteorStrike` is `{ impact_position, impact_tick, trajectory_seed, size }`, sent at T minus `METEOR_SHOWER_WARNING_SECONDS` (60 s before the FIRST impact) when the scheduled announce tick arrives. It rides the reliable channel (a dropped announce would leave a player blind to an incoming meteor, see `ServerMessage::delivery`), and `meteor_shower_announce_for` (src/server/meteor_shower.rs - meteor_shower_announce_for) resends the payload with every still-live meteor to any client that connects while the event is alive, announce through the last crater despawn, appended by the connection/welcome path (src/server/connection.rs), so late joiners see the fireballs or craters immediately.

Everything downstream (fireball positions, danger warnings, craters, cleanup) is a deterministic function of that payload plus the client's own authoritative-clock estimate. A meteor is never per-tick replicated: like `ServerMessage::WorldTime`, where clients integrate the clock locally between sparse authoritative signals, a meteor is neither an event stream nor per-entity state, it is a function of time. See the replicated-state rules in CLAUDE.md before considering a replicated meteor entity.

## Shared trajectory and crater math

`src/world/meteor_shower.rs` is pure and dependency-light (only `Vec2`/`Vec3` math plus the shared `splitmix64`), so the client renderer and the determinism tests call the same functions.

- `METEOR_FLIGHT_SECONDS` = 10.0: the visible flight window per meteor. The committed arc spans exactly this window, from the entry point at `impact_tick - 10 s` to the impact point at `impact_tick`. Deliberately short (matched to the `/meteor-here` dev feel): each fireball appears and dives as a fast committed streak, with the crossing rumble starting the moment it appears (`FLYBY_LEAD_S` in `src/app/scene/meteor_shower.rs` ties itself to this constant), so sight and sound arrive together. Distant meteors render with damped drama growth (the `METEOR_DRAMA_*` constants in `src/app/scene/meteor_sky.rs`) and their rumble/boom stay world-audible at distance-scaled volume, capped at the tuned near loudness. The far boom model (all in `src/app/scene/meteor_shower.rs`): inside `BOOM_SPATIAL_RANGE_M` (300 m) the untouched spatial path plays; past it the boom plays non-spatially at `BOOM_FAR_DB_PER_DECADE` (-28 dB/decade, the extra over inverse-distance standing in for air absorption) floored at `BOOM_FAR_FLOOR_DB` (-34), with its detonation peak scheduled to land exactly on the visual impact frame (`meteor_boom_push_delay`; a speed-of-sound travel lag was tried and REMOVED, it split the strike into "trees fell" and then a late "impact", owner report); past `BOOM_MUFFLED_RANGE_M` (800 m), or whenever a nearby collider blocks the listener's bearing to the strike (`listener_sheltered_toward`, local-AoI shelter scan with an extra `BOOM_SHELTER_EXTRA_DB`), the low-passed `world/meteor-impact-far.wav` variant plays so distance and cover genuinely dull the sound.
- **Entry point** is seeded off `trajectory_seed` through `splitmix64` with per-axis salts (`seeded_unit`): a compass azimuth, a horizontal distance of 5000 to 7000 m out, and an altitude of 2500 to 3500 m up, so each object enters from far beyond the world edge (and each meteor of a shower enters from its own bearing).
- **Descent** follows a quadratic Bezier (the control point bows `PATH_BOW_FRACTION` = 0.18 of the entry altitude off the straight chord) reparametrised by a quadratic ease (`u = p * p`), so the final approach visibly accelerates.
- **Velocity** is the analytic derivative of that path (chain rule through the ease), continuous by construction; no finite-difference jitter.
- `meteor_world_state(impact_position, impact_tick, trajectory_seed, estimated_tick)` returns `None` outside the flight window (before `remaining <= METEOR_FLIGHT_SECONDS` and at/after `impact_tick`); inside it, the object is on one committed arc that ends exactly at the impact point. `estimated_tick` is FRACTIONAL on purpose: evaluating at whole 20 Hz ticks quantises the descent into 50 ms position steps that stutter at render frame rates (`fractional_ticks_move_the_meteor_between_whole_ticks` guards this). `size` never enters the trajectory; it scales the renderer and the blast at the call sites.
- The module also owns the shared crater surface profile: `crater_surface_height(distance, size)` plus the `CRATER_*` constants (bowl radius 6.5 m, rim end 9.5 m, skirt 14.5 m, rim height 0.85 m, all at size 1.0). **Size scales the profile as a pure homothety** (radii AND heights), so a size-scaled crater is exactly the unit crater under a uniform scale; the client renders a uniformly-scaled crater mesh and it still matches the analytic surface point for point (`crater_surface_scales_as_a_pure_homothety_of_size` guards this). The client crater mesh, the movement collider's analytic multi-crater floor (`src/controller/grid.rs` - `BlockGrid::floor_height`, fed `CraterFloor { x, z, size }` records, see [docs/movement.md](movement.md)), and the server's crater-node seating all sample this one function.

## Server engine

`src/server/meteor_shower.rs` owns everything authoritative. `MeteorShowerState` on `GameServer` holds `next_announce_tick: Option<u64>` plus `meteors: Vec<ActiveMeteor>` (empty = no live event) and is advanced by `tick_world_events` from `GameServer::tick` (src/server/tick.rs), ordered schedule -> announce -> per-meteor impact -> per-meteor cleanup.

- **Scheduler.** `roll_next_announce_tick` rolls the next event uniformly in `METEOR_SHOWER_INTERVAL_MINUTES_MIN..=MAX` (10 to 20 REAL minutes), measured event start to event start: the reroll on cleanup anchors at the finished event's first-impact-minus-warning, so the ~10 minute crater window does not pad every gap (a minimum roll clamps to "right after cleanup"). The schedule is real time by design: the admin `/time-speed` cheat accelerates only the day/night visual cycle, so spinning the sun does not pull meteors closer together. The announce lands `METEOR_SHOWER_WARNING_SECONDS` before the first impact.
- **Shape of an event.** The event seed rolls a meteor count in `METEOR_SHOWER_COUNT_MIN..=MAX` (pinned to exactly 2). Exactly ONE meteor per event is the size-1.0 headliner; the rest roll seeded sizes in `METEOR_SHOWER_SECONDARY_SIZE_MIN..=MAX` (0.4 to 0.8). Meteor 0 impacts exactly at the end of the warning window; every other meteor's impact tick carries a seeded stagger in `[0, METEOR_SHOWER_IMPACT_STAGGER_SECONDS]` (45 s), so the event reads as a shower rolling in, not a volley.
- **Siting.** `select_meteor_shower_sites` distributes the N sites by ANGULAR STRATIFICATION: the outer ring (beyond `METEOR_SHOWER_SITE_MIN_CENTER_DISTANCE_FRACTION` = 0.35 of the playable radius, inside `PlayableBounds` inset by `METEOR_SHOWER_SITE_BOUNDS_MARGIN_M` = 20 m) is split into N equal angular sectors with a seeded rotation, and each meteor samples up to `METEOR_SHOWER_SITE_CANDIDATES` = 48 candidates within its own sector. A candidate is rejected within `METEOR_SHOWER_BUILDING_CLEARANCE_M` = 60 m of ANY deployed entity (building pieces included), Tool Cupboard claim footprint cell, or ruin footprint, and additionally within `METEOR_SHOWER_INTER_SITE_SPACING_M` = 120 m of an already-accepted site of the same event. Building safety is guaranteed by siting, never by a damage exemption, so the clearance must exceed the size-1.0 `METEOR_SHOWER_IMPACT_RADIUS_M` = 18 m (const-asserted in `clearance_exceeds_impact_radius`; smaller sizes are strictly safer). If every candidate in a sector fails on a saturated map, that sector falls back to the sampled point maximising the worst of the two clearances.
- **Impact.** `resolve_due_meteor_impacts` resolves each meteor exactly once when the clock reaches its own `impact_tick`. Players inside `METEOR_SHOWER_IMPACT_RADIUS_M * size` take Blast damage through `resolve_blast_on_players`: linear falloff from `METEOR_SHOWER_IMPACT_PLAYER_DAMAGE * size` (250 at size 1.0) at ground zero to 0 at the radius edge, per-player armor via `damage_after_armor`, then the shared `apply_player_damage` tail (knockback, correction, death/loot flow, see [docs/pvp-combat.md](pvp-combat.md)); the headliner's ground zero is lethal through any current armor set. `deplete_nodes_in_radius` removes every resource node in the size-scaled radius through the normal depletion path (regrow schedule plus a `ResourceNodeDepleted` broadcast so clients play the shatter/fell effect). `spawn_meteor_shower_crater_nodes` then scatters the meteorite cluster (`METEORITE_NODE_ID`): the headliner keeps the full `METEOR_SHOWER_CRATER_NODE_COUNT_MIN..=MAX` (3 to 6) roll, smaller meteors scale the rolled count by `size` (minimum 1), seeded off the trajectory seed, seated on the size-scaled `crater_surface_height`, with `METEOR_SHOWER_CRATER_NODE_SPACING_M` = 2.5 m minimum spacing.
- **Cleanup.** At each meteor's `despawn_tick` (`impact_tick` + `METEOR_SHOWER_DESPAWN_SECONDS` = 600 s) `cleanup_expired_meteors` force-despawns that meteor's still-unmined crater nodes (broadcasting `ResourceNodeDepleted`, untracking with NO regrow; these were event spawns, not world nodes) and drops it from the vec. The event slot frees, and the next announce is rolled, only when the LAST meteor cleans up.
- **Deliberately not persisted.** `MeteorShowerState` is transient: on world load the scheduler rolls a fresh next event and an in-flight event does not survive a restart. This keeps the save format untouched (no version bump).
- **Admin commands.** `/meteor [warning_seconds]` (default 30) clears any live event and forces a fresh full multi-meteor shower; `/meteor-here [warning_seconds] [size]` (defaults 8 / 1.0, size clamped to `[0.2, 1.0]`) drops a SINGLE meteor exactly at the caller's position, bypassing siting and the clearance check by design, with the size argument for eyeballing the scaled blast/crater/visuals. Both are admin-gated, and both despawn a replaced event's leftover crater nodes.

## Client presentation

The announce is stored on `runtime.meteor_showers` as a `Vec<MeteorShowerEvent>` (src/app/state/runtime.rs, each entry carrying `size`; the receive path is in src/app/systems/network.rs, which distinguishes a genuinely new announce from a late-join resend by comparing the impact-tick sets). `ClientRuntime::tick_world_time` drops each meteor once its crater window closes on the local clock and keeps the block grid's analytic multi-crater floor (`BlockGrid::set_craters`) in step. Every consumer reads only `runtime.meteor_showers` plus the clock estimate:

- **Fireballs.** The sky pre-spawns `METEOR_SKY_RIGS` = 5 rigs (body layers + trail chain + flame tongues each, tagged `MeteorRig`); meteor `k` of the shower drives rig `k`, placed by `update_meteor_sky_system` (src/app/scene/meteor_sky.rs) evaluating `meteor_world_state` at the fractional `runtime.server_tick_precise()`. A meteor's `size` multiplies the rendered ball radius, so the trail, tongues, ember stream, and per-rig mid-flight airburst all scale with it. Each fireball is a true world-anchored object on the far-plane proxy scheme; rigs without an in-flight meteor stay hidden.
- **Impact sites.** One crater rig per landed meteor (keyed by `impact_tick`), uniformly scaled by `size` (valid because the crater profile is a homothety of size), with size-scaled site fires, the one-time rock blast, per-meteor pre-armed boom audio (gain scaled by size), and the crossing rumble/camera-shake bed driven by the loudest (nearest, size-weighted) in-flight meteor (src/app/scene/meteor_shower.rs).
- **HUD.** ONLY the escalating per-meteor evacuation warning, shown while the player is inside `METEOR_SHOWER_DANGER_RADIUS_M * size` of a not-yet-landed meteor; the most imminent covering meteor drives the escalation (src/app/ui/hud/meteor_shower.rs - meteor_shower_hud). There is no countdown pill and no world-map marker by design; do not add global announcement chrome.

Per-surface detail (draw order, crater mesh construction, audio cue timing) lives in the "Meteor shower: per-meteor warning and sky/ground VFX" section of [docs/ui-and-client.md](ui-and-client.md); do not duplicate it here.

## Balance

The whole `METEOR_SHOWER_*` family lives in the "meteor shower event" block of `src/game_balance.rs`: interval 10 to 20 real minutes, warning 60 s, count exactly 2, secondary size band 0.4 to 0.8, impact stagger 45 s, inter-site spacing 120 m, building clearance 60 m, impact radius 18 m (x size), danger radius 60 m (x size), ground-zero player damage 250 (x size), crater-node count 3 to 6 (scaled by size, min 1), spacing 2.5 m, despawn 600 s, site fires 100 s burn + 30 s fade, 48 site candidates per sector, center-distance fraction 0.35, bounds margin 20 m. Blast knockback shares `EXPLOSION_KNOCKBACK_SPEED` with the explosives family. Per invariant 4 in CLAUDE.md, new meteor tuning goes there, never inline.
